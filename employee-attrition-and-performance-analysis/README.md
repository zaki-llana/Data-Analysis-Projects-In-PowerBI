# IBM Employee Attrition & Performance Analysis 

## Project Overview
This project analyzes a fictional dataset created by IBM data scientists. The dataset contains employee data that can be used to identify patterns in employee attrition and performance. The goal is to uncover key drivers behind employee turnover and evaluate employee performance trends to support data-driven HR decisions.
The analysis was performed on **1.47K employee records** and focuses on identifying high-risk employee groups, department-level trends, and performance distribution.

## Dashboards
- Employee Attrition Analysis
- Employee Performance Analysis

## Objectives
- Identify key factors contributing to employee attrition
- Determine high-risk roles and departments
- Analyze workforce performance trends
- Examine relationships between job satisfaction and performance

## Key Findings & Insights
### Employee Attrition Analysis
- Total employee attrition count reached **237 employees**
- Overall attrition rate was **16.12%**
- **Sales Representatives** showed the **highest attrition rate at 40%**, making them the highest-risk role for employee turnover.
- The **Research & Development department** contributed the highest attrition count with **133 employees**, accounting for the majority of total attrition.
- Employees aged **26–35 years** experienced the highest attrition frequency, indicating a critical retention period.
- **53.59%** of employees worked overtime, suggesting potential workload or burnout concerns.

### Employee Performance Analysis
- The **average performance rating of 3.15** falls below the ideal threshold, suggesting that most employees are only meeting baseline expectations.
- A large performance gap exists, with only **226 employees classified as high performers** compared to **1.24K low performers**.
- **1.24K Low Performers**
- **2.73 Average Job Satisfaction**
- Higher job satisfaction appears to correlate with better performance outcomes.
- Performance variation across departments remained relatively small.

## Business Recommendations
### Attrition Reduction
- Improve retention strategies for **Sales Representatives**
- Investigate causes of turnover within the **Research & Development department**
- Introduce workload balancing initiatives to reduce overtime-related burnout
- Create targeted retention programs for employees aged **26–35**

### Performance Improvement
- Establish employee development and training programs
- Implement recognition and incentive systems for high performers
- Improve employee engagement and job satisfaction initiatives
- Monitor employee performance trends regularly

## Conclusion
The analysis revealed significant workforce trends affecting employee retention and productivity. Attrition risk is concentrated within specific job roles and departments, while employee performance distribution indicates opportunities for workforce development.
These insights can help HR teams create targeted interventions to improve employee retention, increase performance levels, and support overall organizational growth.
